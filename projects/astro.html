SEDS Observatory Trip, 12/4/18

M37 is a globular cluster in the Auriga constellation. It is estimated to contain upward of 500 stars.
This image looks at the core of M37, showing a greater multitude of stars.
M42, the Orion Nebula, is about 400 pc away from Earth in the constellation Orion. This nebula is notable as a study in brown dwarfs, protoplanetary disks, gas motion and star formation.
This image of the Orion Nebula was exposed twice as long, giving far more detail. Additional stars can be seen through the nebula gas now.
NGC 1068, or Messier 77, is one of the earliest Seyfert galaxies discovered (1780) and has been observed to contain supernova.
Uranus is notable for being flipped on it's side, with it's axis rotated 90 degrees.
